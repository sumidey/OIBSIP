Subhash Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was one of the most prominent leaders of the Indian independence movement against British rule. Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, he completed his education in England and then returned to India to participate in the freedom struggle.


Netaji was a charismatic leader who inspired people with his revolutionary ideas and fearless attitude. He was a man of action and believed in using force against the British. He was known for his military tactics and strategies, which he used to great effect in his fight against the British.


In 1928, he was elected as the President of the Indian National Congress, but he resigned from the post after a disagreement with the party's leadership. He formed his own party, the Forward Bloc, to continue the struggle for independence.


Netaji's famous slogan "Give me blood and I will give you freedom" reflects his unwavering commitment to the cause of Indian independence. He was a man of great vision, and he believed that India needed to be free not just from British rule, but also from the shackles of poverty, illiteracy, and social inequality.


In 1941, Netaji left India and went to Germany, where he sought help from the Nazis in his fight against the British. He then went to Japan, where he formed the Indian National Army (INA) with the help of the Japanese. The INA fought alongside the Japanese against the British in Burma and India.


Despite facing many challenges and obstacles, Netaji continued to work towards the goal of a free India. He believed that the only way to achieve independence was through force, and he was willing to do whatever it took to achieve that goal. His contribution to the freedom struggle is immeasurable, and his legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians to this day.


On the occasion of his birth anniversary, we pay tribute to Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose, a true hero and patriot, who dedicated his life to the service of his country and its people. His life and legacy are an inspiration to all of us, and we must strive to uphold the values of freedom, justice, and equality that he stood for.

Important Quotes of Netaji

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was a prolific speaker and writer, and there are several important quotes that he is known for. Here are some of his most famous quotes:


1. "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"


2. "Freedom is not given - it must be taken."


3. "It does not matter who among us will live to see India free. It is enough that India shall be free and that we shall give our all to make her free."


4. "No real change in history has ever been achieved by discussions."


5. "The greatest tragedy of my life is not the loss of eyesight but the loss of the vision."


6. "Soldiers who always remain faithful to their nation, who are always prepared to sacrifice their lives, are invincible."


7. "We should have but one desire today - the desire to die so that India may live - the desire to face a martyr's death, so that the path to freedom may be paved with the martyr's blood."


8. "One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives."


9."Forget not that the grossest crime is to compromise with injustice and wrong. Remember the eternal law: you must give, if you want to get."


10. "It is only on the basis of undiluted nationalism and of perfect justice and impartiality that the Indian Army of Liberation can be built up."
